What is Mental health

Mental health is the state of our emotional, psychological, and social well-being.

  • Emotional Well-Being: A key component of mental health having an impact on the perception and response to the environment, relationships and experiences.

    Involves being aware of and accepting our feelings, and having the ability to manage them effectively, maintaining a positive outlook.

  • Psychological Well-Being: A crucial role in shaping mental health, influencing resilience, self-esteem, and overall satisfaction with life..

    Including having a sense of purpose, being able to manage stress, and having a positive outlook on life.

  • Social Well-Being: A critical role in mental health, as humans are inherently social beings who thrive on connection, communication, and support through building healthy relationships.

    Refering to the quality of your relationships, sense of belonging, and the ability to interact positively with others.

Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.

Identifying Mental Health

Mood swings

Sudden and intense shifts in emotional states, are a common experience in mental health. How individuals or those around them respond to these fluctuations can greatly influence their overall well-being and recovery.

Here are some positve and negative responses to mood swings:

  • ✓ Recognizing mood swings as they occur and identifying triggers.
  • ✓ Writing down thoughts and feelings to process emotions constructively.
  • ✗ Ignoring/hiding emotions instead of addressing them.
  • ✗ Responding impulsively with anger, frustration, or panic to emotional shifts

Social Changes

Withdrawal, aggression, or uncharacteristic habits, can signal mental health challenges. How individuals or their support network respond to these changes can either hinder or promote recovery and emotional well-being.

Here are some positve and negative responses to social changes:

  • ✓ Creating a judgment-free space where the individual feels comfortable being themselves.
  • ✓ Connecting with trusted individuals, such as friends, family, or a therapist.
  • ✗ Pretending the changes are not happening or dismissing them as temporary.
  • ✗ Avoiding conversations or distancing oneself from the individual.

Learn more
Physical Changes

Fluxuation in appetite, sleep patterns, energy levels, or physical appearance, are common. How these changes are responded to can significantly influence a person's mental health and recovery journey.

Here are some positve and negative responses to physical changes:

  • ✓ Activities like walking, healthy meal planning, or stress-relief exercises
  • ✓ Document positive changes to motivate yourself further
  • ✗ Pretending not to notice significant physical changes like fatigue or restlessness.
  • ✗ Focusing only on how the changes affect appearance rather than overall health.

Learn more
Negative Thinking

Self-criticism, pessimism, or catastrophizing, can significantly impact mental health. Responses to negative thinking influence how individuals manage these thoughts and their overall emotional well-being.

Here are some positve and negative responses to negative thinking:

  • ✓ Focuses on actionable steps rather than dwelling on negativity.
  • ✓ Accepting the past can't change but the future is still changeable
  • ✗ Avoiding conversations about their negative thoughts.
  • ✗ Agreeing with their negative thoughts excessively and being too critical on yourself

How Do We Prevent Mental Health From Declining

  • Engage in Enjoyable Activities: Pursuing hobbies that bring joy, fulfillment, acomplishment can serve as therapeutic escapes from stress and anxiety.

  • Stay connected with people you trust: In the times of turmoil having people to support you can act as a positive step towards being better. They don't have to be family, friends, or even a therapist. Just Someone you can trust.

  • Adopt and monitor a healthy lifestyle: Improving nutrition, regular physical activity time and quality of sleep can support cognitive function, emotional well-being, and overall health so tackling challeneges becomes easier.

  • Getting help early: Mental health conditions can be harder to treat if you wait until symptoms get bad. Long-term maintenance treatment also may help prevent a relapse of symptoms.

  • Build resilience: Developing a conflict-resolving attitude promotes the viewing of setbacks, major or minor, as opportunities for growth and learning. This can help you to adapt to change and bounce back from adversity.
